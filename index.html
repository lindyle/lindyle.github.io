Hi! I'm Lindy.

I design and build technologies to support emotional resilience and social connection.

My research focus is on Human-Centered AI to promote inclusion and sustainable structural change. I am particularly interested in leveraging Affective Computing and Social Computing to facilitate mindfulness, compassion, and dialogue. I am currently a product manager at Microsoft, where I also collaborate with Microsoft Research's Human Understanding and Empathy (HUE) group to research empathic AI and mental health. My approach to Human-Computer Interaction is transdisciplinary, inspired by neurodiversity and trauma-informed care. Influenced by Critical Computing and Social Justice, I am committed to conducting community-based participatory action research that centers marginalized voices and intersectionality.

Previously, I have built assistive technology at Microsoft for people with visual, mobility, and cognitive disabilities. In my free time, I love making art through needlework (e.g. knitting, crochet), music (e.g. ocarina, ukuele), and dance (e.g. aerial acrobatics, ballroom).
