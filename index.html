Hi! I'm Lindy.

I design and build technologies to support social-emotional connection.

My research focus is on Human-Centered AI to promote inclusion and sustainable structural change. Motivated by my experiences with neurodivergence, I am particularly interested in leveraging Affective Computing and Social Computing to facilitate mindfulness, compassion, and dialogue. I have been a product manager at Microsoft for 5 years, where I currently collaborate with Microsoft Research's Human Understanding and Empathy (HUE) group to research empathic AI and emotional well-being. My approach to Human-Computer Interaction is transdisciplinary, inspired by neurodiversity and trauma-informed care. Influenced by Critical Computing and Social Justice, I am committed to conducting community-based participatory action research that centers marginalized voices and intersectionality.

Previously, I have built assistive technology at Microsoft for people with visual, mobility, and cognitive disabilities. In my free time, I love making art through needlework (e.g. knitting, crochet), music (e.g. ocarina, ukuele), and dance (e.g. aerial acrobatics, ballroom).

Digital Empathy Research

Currently, I am working with the HUE group to develop a human-centric benchmark for measuring digital empathy in human-AI interactions. The goal is to publish data sets and tools that promote more human-centered AI research.

Esa: Emotional Support Agent (Esa)

Previously, I collaborated with the HUE group on a low-cost, intelligent stress-reduction application. The system detected stress using passive laptop sensors and delivered personalized, just‑in‑time mental health interventions via an embodied agent.
